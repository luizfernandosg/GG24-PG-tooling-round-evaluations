## üîπ **UX Impact**

### **UX Impact Score: 18/20**

### **Rationale:**

The UX problem is clearly defined: Ethereum's coordination layer lacks a verifiable and community owned knowledge base for evaluating projects and making informed collective decisions. Information about builders, projects, and funding impact is fragmented, unverifiable, and centrally curated, creating information asymmetry, trust gaps, and inefficiencies. Without a decentralized process for fact verification and consensus, collective intelligence in Web3 remains underutilized and vulnerable to misinformation.

Severity and evidence are strong - the narrative clearly articulates how information asymmetry hinders collective decision-making, centralized curation creates trust gaps, and lack of decentralized fact verification prevents effective coordination. The proposal addresses Ethereum's core UX challenge in collective sense-making.

The test/validation plan mentions Perennial Grant Campaign using Elderwand rankings and active alpha with 100+ projects, but success metrics could be more specific. The proposal should define KPIs such as evaluations performed, projects using Elderwand metrics, or funding decisions influenced.

Impact potential is high - addresses fundamental coordination challenge, enables better collective decision-making, and transforms fragmented data into open, continuously verifiable public knowledge layer. Overall assessment is strong - clear problem with strong narrative and validation plan.
