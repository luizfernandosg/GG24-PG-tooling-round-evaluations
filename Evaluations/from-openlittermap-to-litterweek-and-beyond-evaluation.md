## Key Aspects

### Integration/Development in Question
Build on OpenLitterMap to launch "LitterWeek" campaign and related public-goods initiatives. Deliverables include: (1) Launch OLMv5 - major AI-transformation of OpenLitterMap (ongoing PR #668); (2) Build LitterWeek MVP and get first users; (3) Revisit Littercoin smart contract, get it audited, and run testnet experiments. The proposal focuses on creating a modern internet onboarding experience that teaches people how to use new technologies through citizen science litter mapping.

### Funding Amount
$10,000 requested. Budget breakdown not detailed in proposal - proposer mentions hiring developers and community/socials/strategy roles with this funding. The proposal mentions a pre-seed target of $420,069, with ~$20,000 raised from Cardano and $5,000 from Gitcoin previously.

### General Impression
Strong, mission-aligned project with demonstrated traction in citizen science and open data. OpenLitterMap is a recognized Digital Public Good by the UN-endorsed Digital Public Goods Alliance, with 8,763+ users organically signed up. Clear community/public benefit and education angle. However, technical interoperability plans and precise budget/timeline require clarification. The proposal mentions Karma GAP integration but doesn't specify concrete integrations with 2+ recognized primitives.

### Alignment with Round
- Interoperability: Weak - only mentions Karma GAP integration, no concrete plan for 2+ primitives
- Collaboration: Weak - mentions 8,763+ users and Uganda initiative but no named partners or evidence
- UI/UX: Moderate - addresses onboarding and education UX through LitterWeek campaign

## üîπ **UX Impact**

### **Rationale:**

The UX problem is implicitly defined: society is missing a modern internet onboarding experience that can help teach people how to use new and better technologies. The proposal addresses this through LitterWeek campaign that engages people in producing data to unlock in-world educational experiences in technology and the environment.

However, the problem is not explicitly framed with clear severity evidence. Impact potential is high if onboarding friction is reduced and campaigns scale, but the validation plan needs more specificity.

Overall assessment is moderate - strong social impact narrative but weak on explicit problem framing and validation metrics.
